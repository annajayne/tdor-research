Albert Rudisill's body was discovered hanging from a beam in the warehouse where they worked in Charlotte, South Carolina on the morning of 23 Apr 1932. 

Rudisill was dressed in woman's clothing, and woman's underwear had been forced into their mouth, pushing Rudisill's tounge back into their throat. Police initially believed Rudisill had committed suicide, but after further investigation concluded it was a murder that had occured the previous day (22 Apr 1932). Despite a prolonged investigation, and multiple suspects, no arrests were ever made.
