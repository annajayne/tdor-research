> Rebeca Lopes, friend of trans woman Mayla Rafaela Martins, 22, stabbed to death on Tuesday (16), in Lucas do Rio Verde (333 km from Cuiabá), stated that businessman Jorlan Cristiano Ferreira, 44, was obsessed with the young woman and chased her. 
>  
> Myala's body was found wrapped in a swimming pool tarp on the banks of the MT-485, on the Morocó road, between Sorriso and Lucas do Rio Verde, by workers. Upon being detained, Jorlan informally claimed to the police that he was robbed by the woman when he opened his establishment in the city. 
>  
> During Mayla's wake, this Thursday morning (18), in Várzea Grande, the young trans girl's friends gathered to say goodbye, demanding justice. Rebeca, who had known the woman for eight years, said that Jorlan premeditated the crime, as he kept threatening her. 
>  
> "This man, in addition to committing this barbarity with my friend, he premeditated, he murdered her, hid her corpse and disappeared with her cell phone. He had been having romantic encounters with her since the beginning of December. Since his wife traveled , he took her home, they had weekly meetings. There are messages, prints of her saying all this", he declared in an interview with journalists. 
>  
> "We want justice. Even after she's dead, he's framing my friend as a thief and saying she extorted him. Mayla didn't do that, she didn't carry a knife. She worked and took the money home," he added.
>  
> The friend also revealed that Mayla was afraid that something could happen because of the businessman's current behavior. In exchanges of messages with Rebeca, the victim, who worked as a sex worker, said that she had bought a ticket to return to Cuiabá, in order to escape the criminal's persecution, but was murdered first. 
>  
> "He chased her. He went to where she worked, he already told her not to stay on the street anymore, he was too jealous of her, he was obsessive. She bought a ticket to leave [to Cuiabá], but she didn't come. 
>  She knew that one day this was going to happen, due to his threats. He had threatened her, because he didn't want her to leave. He was obsessed with her", he said. 
>  
> Mayla was buried this Thursday (18), in Várzea Grande. The young woman's sister used social media to fundraise in order to transfer the body. 
>  
> The young woman lived in the metropolitan city, but moved to Lucas do Rio Verde in December last year, to work. The objective was to save money to go to Europe. 
>  
> As reported by Olhar Direto , Mayla's body was found on a farm, between Sorriso and Lucas do Rio Verde. 
> 
> After the incident, investigators went to the victim's house, where they obtained important information from a friend of hers.  
>  
> Hours before she was killed, Mayla left a gas station with Jorlan, in a VW Polo Sedan vehicle. Before getting into the car, she sent a photo of the license plate to her friend.  
>  
> Based on the license plate, the police came up with the name of the suspect, who has a hamburger restaurant in Lucas do Rio Verde. 
>  
> Jorlan Cristiano claimed he attacked the victim following a financial disagreement related to a previous alleged robbery. Information appears in a document prepared by the Civil Police after the crime. 
>  
> The delegate responsible for the case, João Antônio Ribeiro, however, does not believe the version presented by the businessman. According to the police authority, the Official Expertise and Technical Identification (Politec) will analyze whether the young transsexual was sexually abused before being killed.



> Judge orders businessman preventative for killing trans people: “cold and violent”
> Magistrate highlights that the murder was carried out by a hateful outfit
> 
> Judge Fábio Petengill, from the 2nd Criminal Court of Lucas do Rio Verde, classified businessman Jorlan Cristiano Ferreira, aged 44, confessed perpetrator of the murder of young transsexual Mayla Rafaela Martins, aged 22, as cold, calculating and extremely violent. The information is contained in the decision of this Wednesday's custody hearing (17), which converted Jorlan's arrest into preventative arrest. The man confessed to stabbing the young woman to death in the backyard of his house and then placing the body on an old pool canvas and dumping it in a soybean field.
> 
> He claims that he committed the crime because the victim was trying to extort him, a version that is being investigated by the Civil Police. 
> 
> "Whether Mayla's death was the result of a vendetta designed by the custodian or was due to an outburst of hatred by a man who, during the early hours of the morning, was looking for sexual satisfaction is something that is still unclear, but what is certain is that the action, regardless of the motivation, both before and after the crime, revealed a cold, calculating and extremely violent personality, which demonstrates its relevance in guaranteeing the application of the law and, especially, in guaranteeing public order, given the concrete gravity of the criminal act", brings a part of the decision.
> 
> The Military Police became aware of the crime after being called by workers who had located a body at Fazenda Rio Verde last Tuesday (16), with the victim identified as Mayla. Then, a team went to the victim's residence, where they were informed by a friend of Mayla's that she had gone out to do a sexual activity with someone and reported the license plate number of the car that picked up the young woman. Given the information, the police investigated and discovered that the owner of the vehicle was the attacker's wife. The agents then went to the custodian's commercial establishment (Curitibanos Lanches), where in conversation with Jorlan, he confessed that he got into a physical fight with the victim, after Mayla apparently tried to extort him. 
> 
> In the decision that keeps the killer in prison, judge Fábio Petengill highlights that after the crime Jorlan took the car to a car wash to try to get rid of traces and evidence of the crime. "His skillfulness, his 'calmness' in carrying out all these maneuvers, including using his wife's vehicle and not his own, a vehicle that was later sent to a car wash, demonstrate the concrete danger of the agent, which, combined with the crime of concealing a corpse, also shows that he had no intention of handing himself over to the police authority and intended to bury the crime with the 'disappearance' of the victim, demonstrating the degree of concrete risk the agent poses to the social order and to the application of criminal law", points out another part of the decision.

> Mayla Rafaela Martins was a young trans woman who was stabbed to death
> 
> Young trans Mayla Rafaela Martins, aged 22, was murdered in Lucas do Rio Verde and the main suspect for committing the crime is businessman Jorlan Cristiano, who is in prison. 
> 
> She was very happy, fun, hardworking, she chased her dreams, her goals, she was a dreamy girl
> Mayla was affectionately described by her family as a happy and dreamy person.
> 
> “She was very happy, fun, hardworking, she chased her dreams, her goals, she was a dreamy girl,” said Letícia Martins, 32 years old, the victim's sister.
> 
> The young woman's most latent dream was to move abroad and work to help her family.
> 
> “Unfortunately, this dream was ended. She wanted to earn her money, live abroad to help her family. She always talked about the United States,” she said.
> 
> Mayla was wrapped in a plastic pool and abandoned on the side of the highway, between the cities of Sorriso and Lucas do Rio Verde. Her body was found by rural workers on Tuesday morning (16).
> 
> According to Letícia, her sister was an escort and served people with high purchasing power in rural cities.
> 
> The young woman frequently made these trips for work and had been in Lucas do Rio Verde for around a month and a half.
> 
> “It seems that she had already bought a ticket to come home, but unfortunately she didn't arrive.”
> 
> “She went, made money and came back, she went, made money and came back. So much so that with this she even bought a house here in Várzea Grande”.
> 
> According to Letícia, the young woman would leave the house to her younger sister when she moved abroad.
> 
> On Tuesday night (16), businessman Jorlan Cristiano was arrested and confessed to having committed the crime.
> 
> Letícia said that she did not know of any threat suffered by her sister, but that Mayla even took a photo of the businessman's car license plate and sent it to a friend.
> 
> The photograph would have helped the Police find Jorlan's whereabouts.
> 
> “This person who went out with her seems to be her client, who was threatening her, so much so that when she got into the car she took a photo of the license plate,” he said.
> 
> Mayla was from Cuiabana and lived in Várzea Grande, where the body will be laid to rest.
> 
> The family asked for help on social media to cover the costs of moving her body.
> 
> “We are from a humble family, we spent R$9,000, as she had a lot of acquaintances and friends, we crowdfunded and raised a little bit of each.”
