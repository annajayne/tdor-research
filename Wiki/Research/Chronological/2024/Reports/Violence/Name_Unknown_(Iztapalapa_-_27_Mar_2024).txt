> The red one 03/28/2024 09:38 Eloy Linares Updated09:38

> MEXICO CITY.- A corpse with exposed organs and a dismembered leg was found in a wooded area of ​​​​Iztapalapa ; residents believe it was a hate crime. 
> 
> At around 7:00 am on Wednesday, residents of the  Miravalles neighborhood found a body with signs of violence in the weeds on the corner of Atlántida and Armando streets. 
> 
> Since the subject could not be identified as a resident of the area, they requested the presence of the police.
> 
> According to a group of people who came to see the body, the man was a member of the LGBTIQ+ community (lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, transsexual, transvestite, intersex and queer) who was murdered because of his sexual preference.
> 
> They also explained that on Monday night the charred body of Agustín, who was known as “Kitty” among members of the community, was found inside a building in the Iztapalapa municipality. 
> 
> After confirming the discovery, police requested the presence of paramedics and personnel from the Prosecutor's Office. 
> 
> After paramedics diagnosed that the man, aged between 20 and 25, no longer had vital signs, agents from the Forensic Services removed the body.
> 
> To determine the cause of death, the body was transferred as unknown to a local morgue where a legal autopsy will be performed.
