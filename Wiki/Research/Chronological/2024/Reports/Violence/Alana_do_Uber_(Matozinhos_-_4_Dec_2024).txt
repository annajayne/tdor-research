> Alana do Uber, murdered this Wednesday, was the first trans woman to run for councilor in Matozinhos
> 
> On Wednesday night (4), Alana do Uber, a 27-year-old trans woman, was shot dead while she was in a restaurant with friends in Matozinhos, in the Metropolitan Region of Belo Horizonte.
> 
> Alana was the first trans woman to run for a seat on the city council. A candidate for the Avante party in the 2024 elections, she received 52 votes but was not elected. She was known for her charisma, humility and for fighting for her ideals.
> 
> A friend who witnessed the crime said in an interview with  Itatiaia  that Alana was “a very good person at heart, energetic and fearless”. He highlighted her determination to occupy spaces of representation in local politics.
> 
> Ongoing investigations
> Authorities have not yet released details about the motives behind the crime, which is still being investigated by the Civil Police. Alana's death has caused consternation and sparked discussions about the challenges faced by the LGBTQIA+ population in their search for representation and respect.
> 
> Sources:  Amanda Antunes and Vinícius Brito | Itatiaia



> Young trans woman is murdered in Matozinhos near a church
> 
> On Wednesday night (04), the Military Police (PM) were called to respond to a homicide incident near Praça São Sebastião in Matozinhos. At the scene, the body of Alana do Uber was found with several bullet holes, with no vital signs. The police team isolated the area and called the ambulance, but the medical team confirmed the death at the scene.
> 
> The experts collected .40 caliber ammunition casings and a projectile, and the body was removed to the IML. Witnesses at the bar told the police that, upon hearing the shots, they were frightened and ran to the back of the establishment, but did not see the perpetrator.
> 
> According to police, the establishment's surveillance cameras were out of order due to power outages caused by heavy rain the night before. During the investigation, suspicions arose about two individuals, who were identified as possible perpetrators of the crime. Police teams searched an address where the suspects could be, but were unsuccessful in locating them.
> 
> Security cameras at other nearby establishments, which could have provided footage of the incident, were also inoperative or inaccessible.
> 
> The Civil Police are investigating the crime.





> A trans woman was killed near a church, in Matozinhos, in the metropolitan region of Belo Horizonte, on the night of this Wednesday (04).
> 
> According to the police report, the PM (Military Police) was called to a possible homicide in Praça São Sebastião. According to the military, many people were gathered near the victim's body, which was on the ground with several injuries.
> 
> According to witnesses, when they heard the shots, they ran behind a bar in the area and did not see the shooter. However, one of the complainants present at the scene at the time of the crime told the police that she knew Alana and that the victim was with a friend, but she was unable to say where he was during the crime.
> 
> In conversations with Isaías, the victim's uncle, he reported that Alana mentioned a possible threat in a post on a social network, where she said that "he'll cut down people of my race with bullets", referring to her father's words. The relative also reported that there could be drugs in the woman's house. The military went to the location, but found nothing.
