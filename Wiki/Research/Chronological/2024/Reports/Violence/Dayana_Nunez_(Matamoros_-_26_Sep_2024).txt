> Matamoros, Tamaulipas.- The lifeless body of a young transsexual was recently found in a vacant lot in the Juntas de Aguas neighborhood in Matamoros.
> 
> Jonathan, known as “Dayana,” was 29 years old and worked as a security guard.
> 
> The young trans man's body showed signs of violence, including blows to the skull, which were possibly the cause of death.
> 
> In light of this event, the LGBT community demands justice for Dayana.
> 
> The activist and representative of the LGBT community shared that they intend to make a careful call to the corresponding authorities to follow up on the investigation into Dayana's murder and punish whoever is responsible.



> The mother of Jonathan Ricardo Núñez, a young transsexual murdered in Matamoros , is asking for justice for the tragic death of her son and is also asking for the community's support to cover funeral and cemetery expenses.
> 
> In an interview, María Nieves, the young man's mother, confirmed that Jonathan was only 29 years old. He was known as “Dayana” in the LGBT community and lived in the Vista del Sol sector. María explained that, at that time, her son was not working, since the company where he worked as a security guard had given him a break.
> 
> Regarding the events, he mentioned that the last time he saw his son was the night he left home at 9:00 pm to meet with friends at the Las Américas market.
> 
> Close friends Brayan and Pilar told him that they saw a man take Jonathan away, apparently in the middle of a struggle. That was the last time they heard from him, until he was found dead the next day.
> 
> “I demand justice. My son didn't mess with anyone. I start to think about how much he must have suffered.”
> 
> The affected mother shared the story. She also said that Jonathan did not have a partner after his body was found in a vacant lot with injuries to the skull and other parts of the body, allegedly caused by a block.
> 
> For this reason, the events are being investigated by the Investigative Police of the Attorney General's Office of Tamaulipas (FGJT) in Matamoros.
