====== Mayte Lopez Regalado (Tehuantepec - 1 Oct 2024) ======
Created Wednesday 02 October 2024
@violence


Mayte López Regalado
1 Oct 2024
"Rancho Llano", Highway 185, between Juchitán and Tehuantepec
Tehuantepec
Oaxaca
Mexico
shot


> The lifeless body of Mayte López Regalado, a member of the Sexual Diversity of Juchitán, Oaxaca, was found on Tuesday afternoon in the area of the federal highway known as “Rancho Llano,” between Juchitán and Tehuantepec.
> 
> According to information from the authorities, it was reported that the body of the trans woman showed signs of violence and had been abandoned at the aforementioned location.
> 
> Mayte López Regalado was queen of the Vela de las Auténticas Intrépidas Buscadoras del Peligro, an association dedicated to defending the rights of the Muxe community in Juchitán.
> 
> The muxe community has taken a stand on this atrocious crime and is demanding justice for this unfortunate event, as well as for other murders committed against members of the community, recorded in the Isthmus region and which remain unpunished.




> Mayte LM, a member of the Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Transsexual (LGBT) community, was murdered in the Isthmus of Tehuantepec, Oaxaca.
> 
> The young woman of Juchiteco origin was found on Tuesday morning, with a gunshot wound, next to federal highway 185, Tehuantepec-Juchitán section, a few meters from the junction that leads to the Rancho Llano municipal agency in the neighboring town of San Blas Atempa.
> 
> Her body was found among some bushes and rubbish. Her face was covered, her hands tied behind her back, and her bare feet were tied with tape as signs of the torture she suffered before being killed.
> 
> At the time of her death she was wearing a pink blouse and blue jeans. The corresponding investigation file was opened for this incident.
> 
> As of Tuesday afternoon, the motive for the crime remains unknown, although authorities say they have not ruled out any line of investigation.
> 
> Members of the LGBT community mourned the death and demanded justice.
> 
> "With profound indignation and pain, we at the Trans Indigenous and Afro-descendant Meeting of the State of Oaxaca strongly condemn the murder of our colleague Mayte Matus Regalado," the organizing group announced on the same day.
> 
> "This act of extreme violence reminds us of the vulnerability that trans women face in our society, particularly in regions such as the Isthmus, where discrimination and violence based on gender and identity are exacerbated," they said.
> 
> They therefore demanded a thorough investigation to clarify this "hate crime," achieve justice and end impunity for this community, as well as the implementation of effective public policies that guarantee the security and full exercise of human rights of trans, indigenous and Afro-descendant people in the state.
> 
> "Not one less! Justice for Mayte!" they exclaimed.
> 
> Mayte is the first victim of a "hate crime" against the LGBT community reported in this state, just as Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo's six-year term begins.
