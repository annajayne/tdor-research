> An allegedly Trans person is murdered in Tlaquepaque, the body was located in the Hacienda de Vidrio neighborhood, at the confluence of Orizaba and Córdoba streets, on a property in a ravine with a depth of approximately 3 meters.
> 
> The body was located approximately 12 hours after death; the body apparently had a gunshot wound to the back on the right side.
> 
> The deceased person is wearing a green, blue and white sweatshirt, a denim skirt without shoes, and at first glance there is blood on his legs, the ministerial authorities reported.
> 
> Personnel from the State Prosecutor's Office (FE) carried out the investigations under the National Protocol of Action for Personnel of Justice Prosecution Instances in Cases Involving Sexual Orientation or Gender Identity, to try to clarify the facts.
> 
> The discovery was reported to the emergency number at approximately 2:41 p.m. this Saturday, and was initially attended to by police from the Tlaquepaque Police Station, who cordoned off the place and preserved the evidence and prepared the Approved Police Report (IPH). .
> 
> The extraction of the body was carried out with the support of elements of Civil Protection and Firefighters, paramedics from the Municipal Medical Services.
> 
> Also present at the scene were the Agent of the Public Ministry and the Investigative Police assigned to the Specialized Unit for the Attention of Crimes due to Sexual Diversity of the Special Prosecutor's Office for Human Rights, who carried out the primary investigations.
> 
> At the scene, experts from the Jalisciense Institute of Forensic Sciences established and collected evidence that will be integrated into the investigation folder; The body was transferred to the metropolitan amphitheater where the corresponding rulings will be made, where it is expected that it will be officially recognized by his family.

> The report of the event was received at the emergency number around 2:41 pm today, which led municipal agents to go to the confluence of Orizaba and Córdoba streets.
> 
> In that place, an unconscious person was located in a property in a ravine with a depth of approximately 3 meters, which led to the request for the intervention of emergency bodies .
> 
> The first responders, following instructions from the Prosecutor's Office, proceeded to cordon off the area, preserve the evidence and prepare the Approved Police Report (IPH).
> 
> With the support of Civil Protection and Firefighters, the body was removed.
> 
> The paramedics from the Municipal Medical Services indicated approximately 12 hours of cadaveric evolution and observed an alleged gunshot wound to the back on the right side.
> 
> The Agent of the Public Ministry and the Investigative Police of the Specialized Unit for the Attention to Crimes due to Sexual Diversity of the Special Prosecutor's Office for Human Rights were also present at the scene.
> 
> These teams carry out the initial investigations under the National Action Protocol, with a differential and specialized approach.
> 
> It was reported that the deceased was wearing a green, blue and white sweatshirt, a denim skirt without shoes, and at first glance there was apparently blood-forming fluid on his legs.
> 
> Experts from the Jalisciense Institute of Forensic Sciences collaborated in establishing and collecting evidence that will be integrated into the investigation folder.
> 
> The body was transferred to the metropolitan amphitheater to make the corresponding rulings, and it is expected to be officially identified by his family.
> 
> The State Attorney's Office continues to work diligently on the investigation, reiterating that the information provided is preliminary and that further developments will be released through official channels as they are obtained.
