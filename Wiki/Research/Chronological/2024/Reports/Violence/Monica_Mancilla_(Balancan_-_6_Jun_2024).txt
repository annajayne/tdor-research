> On June 6, in Balancán, Tabasco, Monica Mancilla, aged twenty, was found dead and with bruises on her body on the banks of the Usumacinta River. She was a sex worker.


> June 6, 2024, Balancán Tabasco, the Trans woman #MonicaMancilla is found dead and with signs of violence
> 
> The victim's body was found in the Usumacinta River, the state prosecutor's office did not declare what the main line of investigation is.



> The Herald of Tabasco
> 
> In Balancán, Tabasco, a macabre discovery has shocked the local community. This afternoon, around 7:50 a.m., a male body was spotted floating in the waters of the Usumacinta River.
> 
> This was in the vicinity of the ramp located on calle Josefa Ortiz. The alert about this discovery was given by an anonymous phone call to the municipal authorities, who immediately went to the place, cordoning off the area.
> 
> Agents of the local prosecutor's office were notified and took charge of removing the body, which was transferred to the Forensic Medical Service (SEMEFO) for proper identification and autopsy.
> 
> According to initial reports, the victim, identified as Joel “N,” was carrying a personal identification card, which was found inside his bag and pierced around his neck. The victim’s origin has not yet been determined.
> 
> The tragic discovery took place on the banks of the Usumacinta River, around 6:00 p.m. on Thursday, June 6, 2024, specifically in the area known as the fishermen's "ramp."
> 
> Authorities are carrying out investigations to determine the circumstances of the man's death, determining whether it was a case of drowning or whether he died before falling into the water.
