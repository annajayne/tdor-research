> Rita Pilar Almirón , the trans woman who was the victim of a violent attack by her boyfriend who set her on fire, died this Thursday in the province of Entre Ríos after suffering burns on 90% of her body .
> 
> The incident occurred in the Entre Ríos town of San José, in the Colón department. The 32-year-old victim suffered 90% of her body burns after being attacked by her partner, who took a can, doused her with gasoline and set her on fire.
> 
> According to local press, the victim suffered "multiple organ failure" while hospitalized at San Benjamín Hospital in Colón and died two days after the attack.
> 
> At around 8:30 p.m., the victim managed to reach the sidewalk to scream for help. Neighbors immediately notified the police due to the severity of his injuries.
> 
> Police chief Danilo Parodi said the victim "left the house and asked for help on the street" after suffering second- and third-degree burns.
> 
> Meanwhile, the attacker identified as Carlos Romero , 32, also died as a result of burns. He was the one who doused the woman with fuel, set her on fire with a lighter and hugged her so she could not escape .
> 
> The Colón Departmental Headquarters assured the local press that there was a history of violence between those involved since 2022.



> A trans woman who had been set on fire by her partner in the town of Colón, Entre Ríos province, died yesterday Thursday after suffering burns on 90% of her body. The attacker, who hugged her while she was engulfed in flames and was also hospitalized, died a day after the attack.
> 
> The victim, identified as Rita Pilar, 32, was in critical condition at the Colón hospital and died of multiple organ failure as a result of burns on 90 percent of her body, according to the police report. The attacker, identified as Carlos Romero, who was also hospitalized with serious injuries, died on Wednesday.
> 
> The transfemicide occurred on Tuesday night, around 8:30 p.m., in the house that the couple shared on Remedios de Escalada street at 1200. There, the aggressor doused his partner's body with a can of gasoline, set her on fire with a lighter and hugged her while she was burning in flames.



> The Colón Departmental Headquarters confirmed that on Thursday the 32-year-old trans woman who was hospitalized in the San Benjamín hospital died after being attacked by her partner who doused her with fuel and set her on fire.
> 
> The victim, identified as Rita Pilar, 32 years old, was hospitalized in critical condition at the Colón hospital.
> 
> According to reports, his death was caused by multiple organ failure as a result of the burns he suffered on 90 percent of his body.
> 
> The Public Prosecutor's Office on duty ordered the intervention of the forensic doctor, Dr. Sergio Toscami, to perform the autopsy.
> 
> Last Tuesday night, around 8:30 p.m., officers from the town of San José found Rita Pilar “completely burned” on Remedios de Escalada Street at 1,266, and she told the officers that her partner had thrown gasoline on her with a can, after which he set her on fire and hugged her.
> 
> But he managed to "get away, leave the house and ask for help on the street," according to what Commissioner Danilo Parodi, head of the Police Department, told Elonce at the time.
> 
> The attacker, identified as Carlos Romero, also suffered serious burns and died on Wednesday morning.
