> Attack took place in May 2024. 
> 
> Naina, the guru of Mehak, recounted a harrowing incident in which her chellas were ambushed while returning from a wedding dance function. On Kohat Road, two motorcycles intercepted their car, and the riders began firing in an attempt to stop them. The driver tried to evade the attackers by reversing the vehicle, but the four individuals on the bikes continued firing directly at the car.
>  During the attack, Mehak was struck in the back, and another individual, Kamran, sustained a gunshot wound to his right leg. Fortunately, the driver and the other passengers remained unharmed. Mehak was rushed to Lady Reading Hospital in Peshawar, where she remained hospitalized for two months. Tragically, despite extensive treatment, she succumbed to her injuries.
>  
> An FIR was lodged against unidentified assailants, but no significant action has been taken to date. The lack of progress has left the community grieving and demanding justice for Mehak.
 
 
 
> PESHAWAR: Two transgender persons were injured when unidentified motorcyclists opened fire at them on Kohat Road here on Friday night, the police said.
>
> They said that the transgender persons were returning after performing at a wedding function when four persons riding on motorcycles opened fire at them within the jurisdiction of Rehman Baba police station.
>
> They said that Zahid alias Mehek and Kamran alias Jia, residents of Hashatnagri area of the provincial capital, sustained injuries in the attack and were shifted to the Lady Reading Hospital.
>
> “Their condition is stable and we have launched an investigation into the attack on the transgender persons’ complaint,” Saddar circle DSP Arbab Naeem Haidar told Dawn.
