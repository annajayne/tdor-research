> In a statement, the Mayor of the municipality of Sobradinho-BA, Cleyvinho Sampaio, lamented the murder of civil servant Neuritânia Pacheco. Her body was found this Saturday (14), on a football field, in Vila São Francisco.
> 
> The suspicion is that Neuritânia was stoned to death. Cleyvinho also called for speed in the investigation into the crime.
> 
> The Mayor of Sobradinho, Cleyvinho Sampaio, deeply regrets the tragic death of civil servant Neuritânia Pacheco. Neuritânia stood out for her friendliness and charisma with the public and coworkers and leaves a legacy of dedication and services provided to the community.
> 
> The mayor also emphasizes that he has already requested all efforts and agility from the police authorities in the investigation of this barbaric crime. To the family and friends of our friend Neuritânia, our solidarity and strength in this moment of extreme pain. (Ascom)




> As the Blog had posted last weekend the death of a murder victim whose name is registered as Nerivaldo, the police officially confirmed that the victim is a 48-year-old trans woman found dead in the city of Sobradinho (BA).
> 
> According to reports, the victim's body had signs of violence and there is a suspicion that she was stoned.
> 
> The crime took place on Saturday (14), near a soccer field in Vila São Francisco. According to family members, Neuritânia Pacheco had left home for a romantic date. However, it is not yet known whether this is related to the crime.
> 
> The body of the victim, who was a civil servant at the Sobradinho City Hall, was sent to the Technical Police Department (DPT) of the region. The wake and burial took place on Sunday (15), at the city cemetery.
> 
> The perpetrator and motive for the murder are being investigated by the Sobradinho Territorial Police Station, where the case was registered. The police are awaiting the expert report from the DPT.



> Trans woman found dead in Bahia; police investigate suspected stoning
> Initial information indicates that the victim, who was a civil servant at the Sobradinho City Hall, in the north of the state, was on her way to a meeting when she was murdered. The case is being investigated by the Civil Police (PC).
> By g1 BA and TV São Francisco
> 
> 12/16/2024 5:14 PM
> 
> Trans woman buried after being murdered in northern Bahia
> 
> A 48-year-old trans woman was found dead in the city of Sobradinho, in northern Bahia. According to information given to g1 on Monday (16), the victim's body had signs of violence and there is a suspicion that she was stoned.
> 
> The crime took place on Saturday (14), near a soccer field in Vila São Francisco. According to family members, Neuritânia Pacheco had left home for a romantic date. However, it is not yet known whether this is related to the crime.
> 
> The body of the victim, who was a civil servant at the Sobradinho City Hall, was sent to the Technical Police Department (DPT) in the region. The wake and burial took place on Sunday (15), at the city cemetery, according to information provided by TV São Francisco, an affiliate of Rede Bahia in the region.
> 
> The perpetrator and motive for the murder are being investigated by the Sobradinho Territorial Police Station, where the case was registered. The police are awaiting the expert report from the DPT. As of Monday, no one had been arrested.
> 
> On social media, the mayor of Sobradinho, Cleyvinho Sampaio (PSD), shared a note of condolence for Neuritânia's death, expressing solidarity with the employee's family and friends. She worked as a general services assistant at the Municipal Department of Tourism, Culture and Sports.
> 
> "The Mayor of Sobradinho, Cleyvinho Sampaio, deeply regrets the tragic death of civil servant Neuritânia Pacheco. Neuritânia stood out for her friendliness and charisma with the public and coworkers and leaves a legacy of dedication and services provided to the community.
> 
> The mayor also emphasizes that he has already requested all efforts and agility from the police authorities in the investigation of this barbaric crime. To the family and friends of our friend Neuritânia, our solidarity and strength in this moment of extreme pain."
