> Baltimore (WBFF) — Baltimore Police confirmed Wednesday the identity of one of the city's latest shooting victims--a member of the transgender community.That victim's killer remains on the loose as police grapple with the ongoing violence in the Western District.
> 
> The number of shootings and homicides are trending in the right direction across the city--down.But on the west side, there's a recent and alarming amount of violence.
> 
> Police found someone fatally shot in an alley off North Monroe Street and West Lanvale Sunday morning.Police identifying the victim now as 24-year-old Tai'Vion Lathan, a transgender woman.The family released a photo and is stunned over the deadly shooting.
> 
> Just a block away from that alley is 82-year-old Raymond Jefferson who sits on the block daily, he said, to talk about the neighborhood.Among that talk now, the violence in the area, including Lathan's killing.
> 
> "When you hear that, it kind of irks you a little because you're wondering why?Why did it have to be that," said Jefferson.
> 
> There was another shooting in west Baltimore Tuesday afternoon just after 3 p.m.A man was gunned down on North Carey Street near Cumberland Street.Police say his injuries are so severe, homicide detectives will be investigating the case.
> 
> In July, at one point, there were 9 victims in 12 days with 5 homicides and 4non-fatal shootings.
> 
> On that rash of violence, Mr. Jefferson weighed in with his bold but bleak analysis.
> 
> "No matter what, it's going to be violence.No matter what, it's going to be violence.To control it, then that's where you need to work at it," said Jefferson.
> 
> As residents like Mr. Jefferson, keep watch.
> 
> "The only way anyone can control violence is do the right thing.If you're not involved in it, then you don't have to worry about it coming your way," said Jefferson.
> 
> Meanwhile, in Lathan's case, there's a reward of up to $8,000for information that leads to an arrest and felony charges.You have to call Metro Crime Stoppers at 1-866-7-LOCKUP.You can remain anonymous.
