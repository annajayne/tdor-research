> The LGBT+ community woke up this Saturday, May 25, with the news of the murder of a trans woman who worked inside a beauty salon in the Cuauhtémoc mayor's office, specifically the Centro neighborhood , where elements of the Secretariat of Citizen Security (SSC) and staff arrived. of medical emergencies. Through an information card it was announced that the establishment was located at the intersection of República de Perú and Incas streets. 
> 
> According to reports from the SSC, it was elements of the corporation who were alerted by the operators of the Command and Control Center (C2) of the Historic Center. Once they arrived at the scene, they located a person on the floor with blood stains, so they asked for support from the emergency services. 
> 
> Civil Protection personnel diagnosed the 42-year-old person without vital signs due to a firearm in the rib cage area. According to reports from the authority recovered by the local press, a man on a motorcycle shot the trans woman and subsequently fled towards Incas Street. 
> 
> The Attorney General's Office of Mexico City (FGJ) was notified of the murder so that they could launch the first investigations and open an investigation file. Meanwhile, elements of the SSC are analyzing the security cameras in the area to search for the alleged attacker. 





> A woman from the LGBTQ+ community was shot to death inside a beauty salon where she was working, which is located in the Historic Center of Mexico City.
> 
> The transfeminicide was recorded in an establishment on the corner of República de Perú and Ignacio Allende very close to the Central Axis, in the Cuauhtémoc mayor's office, around 8:00 p.m. on Friday, May 24, 2024.
> 
> According to the first investigations by the capital authorities, a subject on a motorcycle approached the scene and executed the victim by shooting several times.
> 
> After the crime, the Mexico City police began locating the culprit for his arrest through video surveillance cameras. Meanwhile, the area was protected to continue with the corresponding investigations by experts from the capital's Prosecutor's Office to determine responsibilities.
