> Reyna Hernandez, 54, went missing on Feb. 26, 2024 from her home in Renton, WA USA. On Mar 8, 2024 her body was found in a cemetery in Mexicali, Baja California Mexico. Reyna was a popular salon owner. 
> Descanse en paz, Rayna.



> The body of a transsexual woman who was found at the beginning of March in the El Centinela pantheon was a resident of the United States, reported the state attorney general, María Elena Andrade Ramírez.
> 
> He indicated that there is a detainee for committing a federal crime, however his car matches the vehicle they used to leave the body in said area, it is mentioned that she was apparently deprived of her freedom in the neighboring country.
> 
> On one side of the body they located several black caps; At the time of the subject's arrest, inside the vehicle they found the same caps.
> 
> “We have some strong lines of investigation, especially the rapprochement with the North American authorities,” said the prosecutor.
> They have not yet established the motive for why she was murdered, they only know that she was a transsexual person who had cosmetic operations.
> 
> "Right now we are verifying what the issue may be, but if so and it is a crime that was committed in Mexico and according to the criteria of the Supreme Court it could be a feminicide."
>
> The events were recorded on March 2 of this year at 1:47 p.m., where at the end of the sentinel pantheon located west of Mexicali, they located a body wrapped in a blanket.
> 
> The body had gunshot wounds to the head.
> 
> As particular features, it had tattoos of a dog's head on the back of the chest, three roses with a crown, a relaxation on the left arm, a female figure in a red dress, the legend of "Alex" on the right leg, among others.



> Mexicali, Baja California.- The trans woman who was murdered this weekend in a lonely place in the “El Centinela” pantheon remains unidentified in the morgue of the Forensic Medical Service, in Mexicali.
> 
> The cause of death was established as a wound caused by a firearm projectile penetrating the skull, according to the autopsy report.
> 
> His body corresponds to that of a man between 40 and 45 years of age , although due to clothing and other physical signs, it is presumed that he is a trans woman.
> 
> As particular signs for his identification, it was noted that he has a tattoo with the head of a dog on his back, three roses with a crown, a watch on his left arm, a female figure in a red dress, the word “Alex” on the outside of right leg, among others.
> 
> The victim's body has a semi-robust build and had a red wig , his hands and feet were tied with a nylon rope, in addition to being wrapped in a blanket next to a mesquite tree.
> 
> A total of 17 black caps with the same logo were found near the body, although it is unknown if they are related to the crime as evidence.

 
> Mexicali, BC. Tied hand and foot to a tree, the body of a transgender woman was located in the El Centinela pantheon in Mexicali.
> 
> The police report indicates that the victim was wrapped in a white blanket, has a tattoo of an apple on her right forearm and multiple signs of torture, especially on her swollen face and bruises throughout her abdomen.
> 
> When warning about violence against women on this border, the leader of the Ibero-American Human Rights Network, Meritxel Calderón, warned about hate murders, whose motive is discrimination, as is the case of transfemicides, recognized even by the Supreme Court. Court of Justice of the Nation.
> 
> At the scene where the victim was found, 15 black caps with pink and green seals were scattered.
> 
> The discovery was made this Saturday morning in the cemetery located on the Mexicali-Tijuana highway near La Rosita.
> 
> The body was discovered by a man who went to the cemetery on Saturday morning, around 9 a.m., when he saw the wrapped body and where his face and long red hair were shown.
> 
> Later, forensic personnel confirmed that the victim was a transsexual woman.
> 
> At the time, the activist said in an interview that judges and public ministries act with pretenses of power and without gender perspective.
> 
> He said that the actions to comply with the gender alert were made visible but they remained on paper and are insufficient because a hostile environment towards women remains in the streets and femicides, murders of women and hate crimes due to discrimination continue. specifically those committed against the trans community.
