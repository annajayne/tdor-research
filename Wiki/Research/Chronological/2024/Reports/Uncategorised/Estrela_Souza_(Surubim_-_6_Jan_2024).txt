> Estrela Souza, known as Telinha, was 35 years old | Photo: Reproduction from social networks
> A 35-year-old trans woman was found dead in a dam located on the Cai-Aí River, in Sítio dos Pinhões, Rural Zone of Surubim, in Agreste de Pernambuco. The place is known by locals as “Dagata Dam”.
> 
> The young trans woman was known as Estrela Souza, 35 years old. She would have left the house at around 1:30 pm and, around 4:00 pm, she was found dead, according to initial information from witnesses.
> 
> The case is considered by the Civil Police as a “death to be clarified”. Estrela's body, or “Telinha”, was located this Saturday (6) and sent to the Caruaru Institute of Legal Medicine for examination. There were signs of drowning, but it needs to be confirmed, as well as whether the drowning was natural.
> 
> The Civil Police reported that they will provide further clarification “at an appropriate time”.
> 
> The fire team was called to remove the body from the dam, but it was removed before the drowning site, it is not known how. "There was a call to remove a body from this location. However, when the garrison was on the way, it was dismissed", says an excerpt from the note. 




> A trans woman was found dead in a dam in Surubim, in the Agreste region of Pernambuco. 
>  
> According to the Civil Police, the victim was 35 years old. 
>  
> The body was in the area known as Dagata Dam and was located on Saturday (6). 
>  
> Unofficial information indicates that the murdered trans woman was known by the nickname “Telinha”.
>  
> In a note, the Civil Police informed that it opened a police investigation to investigate the case.
>  
> The incident was registered on the day “as a death to be clarified”. 
> “More information may be provided at an appropriate time,” added the note.  
>  
> The causes of death have not yet been clarified.
>  
> The body went to the Institute of Forensic Medicine (IML) in Caruaru, also in Agreste. 
> 
> Pernambuco led, in 2022, the ranking of murders of trans people across the country.
>  
> The data is from the National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals (Antra).
> 
> A dossier showed that the state recorded 13 cases of Intentional Lethal Violent Crimes (CVLIs) against trans people. 
> 
> According to the entity, at the time, Pernambuco went from 5th in the ranking to take first place.
>  
> The state was followed by São Paulo, which fell from 1st to 2nd position, and Ceará, which went from 4th to 3rd, in 2022, with 11 cases each.
>  
> Minas Gerais maintained the nine cases from the previous year and Rio de Janeiro, which had 12 murders in 2021, closed 2022 with eight. 
> 
> Amazonas had eight cases and Bahia 7. Paraná, Pará and Espírito Santo had six murders each; Goiás, Mato Grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul and Sergipe with five cases each.
>  
> These states were followed by Alagoas, Paraíba and Maranhão with four murders in each state and Rio Grande do Norte with 3.
> 
> There were also two cases in Rio Grande do Sul, Santa Catarina and the Federal District. A murder in Rondônia, Piauí and Roraima.
