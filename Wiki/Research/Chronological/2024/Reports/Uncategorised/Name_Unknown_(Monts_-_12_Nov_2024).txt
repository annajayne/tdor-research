> The sixty-year-old woman who had been missing for almost two weeks in Indre-et-Loire has been found, unfortunately too late. The body of the victim, a 64-year-old transgender woman, was discovered lifeless on Tuesday, November 12 in the town of Monts (Indre-et-Loire).
> 
> It was one of her relatives who found the sixty-year-old, whose body was "half submerged below a ditch" not far from the town's mill. The electric bike that the victim mainly rode was recovered by the police in the area.
> 
> The investigation is continuing but according to initial findings, no third party was involved in the tragedy. The police have ruled out criminal involvement and are instead assuming a malaise or accident. An autopsy of the body will provide more information. The victim had been missing since October 30 and the police launched an appeal for witnesses on Monday, November 11.
