> Grief and uncertainty grip the community of Santa Marta following the confirmed death of Naomis Redondo Labastida, a 31-year-old transgender woman who died in Spain after falling from the fourth floor of a building. The incident occurred early Saturday morning and is currently under investigation by local authorities.
> 
> According to initial reports, Naomis was in an apartment with her partner when, during an alleged argument, she fell from the balcony. Although she was immediately taken to a private clinic, doctors were unable to save her life due to the severity of her injuries.
> 
> Who was Naomis Redondo, the trans woman who died in Spain?
> Naomis was a beloved figure in the San Martín and Pescaíto neighborhoods of Santa Marta, where she is remembered as a cheerful, hardworking woman full of dreams. She had traveled to Europe months earlier seeking better job opportunities and a more stable life for her family.
> 
> “She dreamed of getting ahead, having stability, and building a peaceful future,” said a close friend from Santa Marta, the capital of Magdalena. The tragic news has deeply affected her loved ones, who are seeking ways to repatriate her body and give her a final farewell in her hometown.
> 
> Spanish authorities are conducting their respective investigations to determine whether the fall was an accident or if there was third-party involvement. So far, no hypothesis has been ruled out, and it is hoped that the results of the legal proceedings will clarify the circumstances surrounding the young woman's death.
> 
> The case has caused shock in both Spain and Colombia, especially among groups that defend the rights of trans people, who have requested that the investigation be carried out with a gender focus and free of prejudice.


> Naomis Redondo Labastida , 31, was identified as the Colombian trans woman who died this Sunday after suffering a fall from a fourth floor in Spain .
> 
> The deceased was originally from the city of Santa Marta , in the Colombian Caribbean, where residents of the San Martín and Pescaíto neighborhoods are now mourning her passing, according to the local media outlet 'Hoy Diario del Magdalena'.
> 
> According to initial reports, the incident occurred in the early hours of last Saturday, apparently during an argument with his romantic partner, information that is being investigated by the authorities of the European country.
> 
> After the fall, Naomis Redondo Labastida was taken to the emergency room of a clinic, where she remained for a day in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) under a reserved prognosis.
> 
> Finally, the woman from Santa Marta died on November 2nd at the medical center due to the severity of the injuries she suffered from the impact.
> 
> According to what Naomis Redondo Labastida's family told the aforementioned media outlet, she had left Colombia and chosen Spain as her new home in the hope of finding opportunities that would allow her to achieve the economic stability she dreamed of.
> 
> Now her relatives are completing the necessary paperwork for the repatriation of her body, as they wish to say goodbye to her in Santa Marta.
> 
> The circumstances surrounding the trans woman's fall are under investigation by Spanish authorities.
