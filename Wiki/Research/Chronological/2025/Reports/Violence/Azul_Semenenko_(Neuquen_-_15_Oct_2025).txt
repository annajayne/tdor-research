> The victim found in Valentina Norte has been identified: it is being investigated as a transfeminicide.
> The Public Prosecutor's Office confirmed that the body found in a canal in Valentina Norte belongs to Azul Semeñenko, a trans woman. The forensic report determined that she died from stab wounds, and the case was classified as transfemicide.
> Thursday, October 16, 2025
> 
> By Alejandra Gonzalez - Journalist - Current Affairs
> 
> The Public Prosecutor's Office (MPF) of Neuquén reported this Wednesday that the person found dead last Tuesday in a canal in the Valentina Norte area has been identified : Azul Semeñenko.
> 
> According to the preliminary autopsy report prepared by the Judicial Branch's Forensic Medical Corps, the victim suffered stab wounds to the chest and arms, as well as fractures to the face, injuries that caused his death.
> 
> When it was confirmed that the woman was trans, the investigation was classified as a transfeminicide.
> 
> Prosecutor Guadalupe Inaudi, from the Prosecutor's Office for Crimes against Persons, is in charge of the case along with the Provincial Police's Personal Security Department.
> 
> During the night, Chief Prosecutor Agustín García, along with Inaudi and María Laura Ciallela, head of the Victim and Witness Assistance Service (SAVyT), met with Azul's family members to provide support and details on the progress of the case.



> #Neuquén A body was found on the side of a canal and they are investigating whether it is a trans woman who has been missing for a month.
> The discovery was made in a canal area, right on the waterfront. A search is underway in the provincial capital for Azul Semeñenko, last seen in September. Another search is also ongoing for a disappearance in July 2024.
> 
> 
> The body of a woman, wrapped in a plastic bag and in a state of decomposition, was found on Tuesday afternoon next to a canal on the outskirts of Neuquén, and they are investigating whether she is Azul Semeñeko , a state employee who has been missing for almost a month.
> 
> The discovery was made Tuesday evening in the Valentina Norte Rural area, a spot on the outskirts of the provincial capital, on the road to Plottier. It was located next to a drain, with part of it in contact with the water, according to the newspaper LM Neuquén.
> 
> The officers who assisted in recovering the body encountered some difficulties due to its poor condition. Prosecutor Lucrecia Sola, who is handling the case, urged caution among local media and indicated that they were working to "determine the identity."
> 
> "In the coming hours, once the Criminalistics police personnel complete their work at the scene, he will be transferred to the Judicial City so that the team from the Judicial Branch's Forensic Medical Corps can perform the autopsy and identification ," judicial sources explained.
> 
> The discovery comes almost a month after the disappearance of Azul Semeñenko, a 49-year-old trans woman who worked as a state employee and was last seen before attending a medical appointment.
> 
> Azul worked at the Victim Assistance Center (CAV), a space dedicated to supporting people in vulnerable situations.
> 
> "We last saw her on Wednesday the 24th. She showed up for work, and on the 25th, which happened to be her 49th birthday, we held a different activity to celebrate the 30th anniversary of the Victim Assistance Center at the Government House. She didn't show up," Evangelina González, a psychologist and coworker, told LM Nequén.
> 
> "She didn't show up for work, even though she usually did it rigorously. Azul is a very responsible woman with her job and would let us know if she was absent," González said.
> 
> On the 25th, around 4:20 a.m., her phone hit an antenna near the Limay River. The search in the area was intensified, however, but as of last week, there were no results. Following her disappearance, marches have been held in recent weeks to demand clarification of the case.
> 
> Added to the search for Azul is the mystery surrounding the disappearance of Luciana Muñoz, a 21-year-old woman who was last seen in July 2024. The two marches complemented each other. "We're not all here: Azul and Luciana are missing," was the cry.
> 
> Luciana left the house she shares with her grandmother, mother, and sister without her cell phone. The courts combed more than 1,000 hectares, viewed more than 800 hours of security camera footage, and ordered 15 raids, but they have not been able to find the young woman. What happened to her remains a mystery.
> 
> An ex-boyfriend was arrested in the case for false testimony, but there is no certainty about what happened to the young woman.
