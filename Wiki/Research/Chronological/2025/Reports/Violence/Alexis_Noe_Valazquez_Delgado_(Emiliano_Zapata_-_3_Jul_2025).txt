> Claman justicia para Alexis Noé, joven trans asesinada en Emiliano Zapata
> Friends and coworkers of Alexis Noé Velázquez Delgado, a 21-year-old trans woman, demonstrated on the Las Trancas-Coatepec highway in Emiliano Zapata to demand that authorities investigate her murder. Her body was found in the Santa Lucía neighborhood after she disappeared on June 28.
> 
> “Her family has already confirmed that they have found Alexis's body, but they haven't given us any further information. Her body was found yesterday afternoon in a neighboring neighborhood where she lived, in Santa Lucía,” one of the protesters commented anonymously.
> 
> She said that so far, her family has not provided any further information; however, they are asking for the case to be clarified so that her murder does not go unpunished. She noted that they had planned to distribute flyers with her name on them to search for her that day, but upon learning of her death, they held the demonstration to demand justice. “We are demanding justice, to clarify the case because he was a very bright child, and it's not fair that his life was cut short at such a young age (21) and that he just remained like that,” she said.
> 
> Upon finding the body in a nearby neighborhood, she considered that the population would also be at risk, so she called for the corresponding alerts to be lifted and for citizen protection to be strengthened. The protesters pointed out that Alexis was a calm person who didn't exhibit problematic behavior, so they have no suspicions about his attacker.
> 
> In this sense, they called on Governor Rocío Nahle to support the case and find the person responsible, as well as to guarantee the safety of the public. “May we be protected, may we have the conviction that each one of us who leaves our homes will return, may our children also have the freedom to walk freely in the streets and that nothing will happen to them. We demand justice for Alexis,” she said.



> Alexis Noé Velázquez Delgado, a 21-year-old trans model and winner of the 2024 Fashion Model Xalapa competition, was found dead on July 2 in the Santa Lucía neighborhood of Emiliano Zapata, Veracruz. She had been reported missing since June 28.
> 
> Her body was found on the night of Wednesday, July 2, in the Santa Lucía neighborhood, near Alexis's home, where he lived in the municipality of Emiliano Zapata. Her family confirmed her identity that same night.
> 
> Alexis disappeared on Saturday, June 28. The State Attorney General's Office has not released official information on the cause of her death.
> 
> Protest in Emiliano Zapata
> 
> On the morning of July 3, family members, friends, and people close to Alexis demonstrated on the Las Trancas–Coatepec highway, near Emiliano Zapata, to demand an investigation with a gender perspective.
> 
> During the demonstration, photographs of Alexis were displayed and the Attorney General's Office was asked not to rule out the possibility of a hate crime.
> 
> The protesters stated that the body was found in an area near Alexis's home, which reinforces their demand for a thorough investigation.
> 
> They requested that the FGE report on the progress of the investigation and that the case be addressed as an act of violence against a trans woman.
> 
> Background of the case:
> 
> Alexis was recognized as the winner of the Fashion Model Xalapa 2024 casting call, making her a role model for other trans women in the region. Her disappearance raised concerns among sexual diversity groups and human rights defenders.
