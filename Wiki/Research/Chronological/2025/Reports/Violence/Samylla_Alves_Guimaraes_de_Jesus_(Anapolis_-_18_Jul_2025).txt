> The Military Police through the 4th BPM – Sant'Ana Battalion, with the support of the Intelligence Agencies of the 3rd CRPM, 4th BPM, 28th BPM and CPE/Anápolis, managed to locate and arrest this Saturday (19), a suspect in the murder of Samylla Morais, a transvestite who worked as a call girl.
> 
> During the operation, police found .32-caliber ammunition, a bloodstained blouse, and other people's documents in the suspect's home. Security camera footage showed that the suspect had arranged to meet the victim shortly before the crime. The car used in the crime was also located and taken for forensic analysis.
> 
> The suspect was taken to the Central Police Station and charged with aggravated homicide, pursuant to Article 121 of the Brazilian Penal Code. In addition to the man arrested, police seized a vehicle for forensic examination, ammunition, and other items recovered during the operation.
> 
> The homicide occurred on Friday night (18), when a transvestite known on social media as Samylla Morais was found dead on a country road near the State University of Goiás (UEG), on the banks of BR-060, in Anápolis. She worked as a call girl in the Calixtolândia region.
> 
> According to witnesses interviewed by police, they heard gunshots during the early morning hours, followed by the sound of a car speeding away. Hours later, Samylla's body was found lying in the vegetation, bearing gunshot wounds, according to forensic evidence.




> Samylla Alves Guimarães de Jesus, a 27-year-old trans woman, was shot dead on Friday night (18) in Anápolis, Goiás, near the State University of Goiás (UEG). The crime occurred on a rural road in the city, approximately 55 kilometers from Goiânia. So far, no suspects have been arrested.
> 
> According to the Civil Police, the crime exhibits the characteristics of an execution. Chief Cleiton Lobo, who is in charge of the investigation, reported that Samylla had posted on her social media accounts the names of clients who had not paid for services provided. Police are investigating whether the perpetrator may have been one of these defaulting clients.
> 
> Witnesses reported hearing gunshots and the sound of a speeding vehicle leaving the crime scene. The Scientific Police conducted an examination at the scene, and the victim's body was sent to the Forensic Medical Institute (IML) in Anápolis. The Homicide Investigation Group (GIH) is leading the investigation.
