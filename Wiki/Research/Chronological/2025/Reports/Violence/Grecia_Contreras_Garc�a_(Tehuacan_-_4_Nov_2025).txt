> A couple was found dead in Tehuacán, and the case is being investigated as a possible hate crime because one of the victims identified as a trans woman.
> 
> They are Grecia Nery and Ángel Emilio Sánchez Rodríguez. Grecia was Miss Trans Veracruz in 2023, and worked as a manicurist and designer. Ángel Emilio was a medical student at BUAP Tehuacán, originally from Veracruz.
> 
> Grecia Nery, 19, previously went by the name Ángel Uriel Contreras García and was in a relationship with Ángel Emilio Sánchez Rodríguez, 21.
> 
> The two were reported missing on November 1, 2025, in the La Purísima neighborhood of Tehuacán. Grecia's family filed investigation file 1612/2025 with the State Attorney General's Office after she failed to return home.
> 
> According to her family, the young trans woman was going out with some friends and her partner, Ángel Emilio Sánchez Rodríguez. Grecia was wearing a cheerleader outfit, apparently because they were going to a party.
> 
> The Specialized Prosecutor's Office for the Investigation of Crimes of Forced Disappearance of Persons and Disappearance Committed by Individuals began the investigations on November 3.
> 
> According to sources from PÁGINA NEGRA, the two young men were found dead inside a house on 5 Oriente and 15 Sur streets in the La Purísima neighborhood, in Tehuacán.
> 
> Preliminary reports suggest this could be a hate crime. Grecia's body was found with signs of asphyxiation, while Ángel Emilio's body appears to have been a suicide. The way the bodies were found suggests that Ángel Emilio may have killed Grecia and then taken his own life.
> 
> The Puebla Prosecutor's Office is currently recovering the two bodies while further lines of investigation are being pursued. Information from those involved in the recovery indicates that there may also have been a suicide note, but this has not been confirmed.




> Tehuacán. The bodies of a same-sex couple were found in a home in the La Purísima neighborhood of this city. The discovery came after their families reported them missing since Saturday, the first of this month, supposedly after they left to attend a costume party. According to unofficial reports, it may be a crime of passion that ended in murder and suicide.
> 
> The deceased are a transgender woman known as Grecia and her romantic partner named Ángel Emilio ; it was reported that she died from mechanical asphyxiation, while the cause of death of the other person is unknown.
> 
> Hours earlier, their relatives launched appeals through social media indicating that they had left their home in the La Purísima neighborhood on Saturday night and hours later lost all communication with them, so they went to the State Attorney General's Office (FGE) from where the corresponding search notices were issued, both with the same investigation file number.
> 
> It was later reported that a patrol car from the Municipal Police and a vehicle from the Forensic Medical Service (Semefo) arrived at a residence in that neighborhood, located at the corner of 5 Oriente and 15 Sur, because the bodies of the young people, Grecia, 19, and José Emilio, 21, had been found inside that building.
> 
> State Police and Auxiliary Police patrols also arrived at the scene to cordon off the area, while FGE personnel were also present to remove the bodies.
> 
> So far, authorities have not released any information, but it has been reported that it may have been a fight between the couple that had a tragic outcome . It is also unknown whether the bodies were discovered as part of the Attorney General's Office investigation or if someone else reported them.
> 
> 
> Gay couple reported missing after attending a costume party in Tehuacán found dead
> The LGBT+ community criticized the authorities for revictimizing the victims and lacking institutional sensitivity by issuing a missing person bulletin without respecting the gender identity of one of the victims.
> November 4, 2025
> 
> After being reported missing on November 1st, when they failed to return from a costume party, a young transgender woman, Grecia Contreras García, 19, and her partner, Ángel Emilio Sánchez Rodríguez, 21, were found dead this Tuesday in a building located on calle 5 Oriente, in the La Purísima neighborhood of the municipality of Tehuacán.
> 
> Relatives of the victims indicated that both were last seen on Saturday and that, after not having communication with them for several hours, the corresponding complaint was filed to activate the search protocols.
> 
> The discovery of the bodies caused shock among family, friends and LGBT+ groups , who since November 1st had been sharing their missing persons posters on social media, as well as images taken inside the nightclub where Grecia was wearing a dress and was accompanied by Emilio.
> 
> After the news was confirmed, Kevin Williams, the representative of the LGBT+ groups of Tehuacán, lamented not only the outcome, but also the way in which the authorities handled the case.
> 
> She pointed out that the search bulletin issued by the Prosecutor's Office failed to recognize the gender identity of one of the victims, Ángel Uriel Contreras, who called himself Grecia, which she described as an act of revictimization and a lack of institutional sensitivity .
> 
> During this Tuesday, elements of the State Attorney General's Office carried out the corresponding procedures at the property and transferred the bodies to the Tehuacán morgue for the pertinent investigations.
> 
> It was reported that one of the bodies showed signs of violence and the other possible signs of suicide ; however, the competent authorities will determine the causes of death.
