> Who was Christina Maciel Oliveira?
> Christina was a renowned trans and transvestite rights activist . She worked on community projects dedicated to health and care within the community , and served as a popular health education agent. Her life was marked by resilience : she was kicked out of her home as a teenager and transformed the violence she suffered into social commitment and support for others.
> 
> “If today I have a roof over my head, a bed to sleep in, food to eat, hot water to bathe in, if I'm no longer homeless, it was thanks to her, the one who encouraged me ,” recalled Gabrielly Elisa, a 21-year-old friend. “ She was brutally murdered , a very cowardly, inhumane thing to do. I hope the authorities don't allow her death, or that of any of us, to become just a statistic,” she said.



> Who was Christina Maciel Oliveira?
> Christina was a renowned trans activist , committed to community work and defending the rights of trans and transvestite people. She worked as a popular health education agent and participated in projects aimed at the care and well-being of the LGBTI+ population in Belo Horizonte.
> During her adolescence, her family evicted her from her home , forcing her to survive in extremely vulnerable situations. Over time, she transformed that experience into a mission to support other homeless trans people.
> Friends and colleagues remember her for her generosity, strength, and social commitment . According to testimonies collected by the G1 portal , Christina was also passionate about cooking, which she used as a means to create spaces for meeting and support among those close to her.
> 
> The social impact and demands for justice in  Brazil
> The murder of Christina Maciel Oliveira sparked a wave of outrage on social media and demonstrations outside public institutions in Belo Horizonte. Activists and human rights organizations demanded a swift, gender-sensitive investigation , pointing out that transfemicide should be treated with the same seriousness that Brazilian law recognizes as hate crimes.
> Her friend, Gabrielly Elisa , 21, stated that the victim was a crucial figure in her life:
> 
> “If I have a roof over my head and a decent life today, it's thanks to her. She was brutally murdered, a very cowardly act. I don't want her death to become just another statistic.”
> The young woman questioned the normalization of violence against trans people in Brazil. This country continues to have one of the highest rates of gender-based murders in the world, according to data from Transgender Europe (TGEU) .


> The Military Police (PM) identified the killer of Christina Maciel Oliveira, 45 years old, trans woman kicked to death in the middle of the street in broad daylight by her ex-partner, on the afternoon of this Monday (20), on Rua Padre Pedro Pinto, in Venda Nova, in Greater BH. The author of the crime is Matheus Henrique Santos Rodrigues, 36 years old.
> 
> The man has several police records for robbery, theft, drug trafficking and consumption, damage, bodily harm and threats.
> 
> Itatiaia has exclusively learned that Matheus Henrique recently left a drug rehabilitation clinic.
> 
> According to what we have learned, Christina wanted to end the relationship she had with Matheus for three years and the man did not accept the breakup, brutally attacking the victim.
> 
> The case will be registered as a femicide. The investigation will be handled by the Specialized Femicide Investigation Unit (NEIF) of the Civil Police's State Homicide Investigation and Personal Protection Department (DHPP).
