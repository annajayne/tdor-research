> Suspect in Grampola's murder identified in São Luís; femicide ruled out
> Civil Police continue investigating the case, in Residencial Ribeira, to understand what motivated the crime.
> By g1 MA — São Luís
> 09/13/2025 08:58  Updated18 hours ago
> 
> The Civil Police reported that they identified a suspect in the murder of the digital influencer known as Grampola, who was stabbed to death in São Luís.
> 
> According to initial information, the crime of femicide has been ruled out.
> 
> Grampola's murder came just days after another brutal crime against a trans woman in the capital.
> 
> The Civil Police reported that they identified a suspect in the murder of the digital influencer known as Grampola, stabbed to death in the early hours of this Friday (12) , in the Ribeira neighborhood, rural area of São Luís.
> 
> So far, no arrests have been made, and the investigation is being handled by the Homicide Division, which initially believes the crime may have been related to drug debts. Therefore, femicide was initially ruled out. However, police say they are continuing to interview family members to understand the true motive for the murder.
> 
> Grampola, whose real name was Inaldo Henrique Silva Belchior, was 41 years old and a trans woman. She became famous in São Luís for the humorous audios that circulated on social media, also known as the "Queen of Audios." The crime occurred on her birthday.
> 
> According to witnesses, the influencer was seen asking for help in a public street, with multiple stab wounds. She was taken to a hospital for treatment, but succumbed to her injuries.
> 
> Grampola's murder came just days after another brutal crime against a trans woman in the capital. On Monday (8), the body of Amanda Falcão, 33, was found in a mangrove area in Aterro do Bacanga.
> 
> The two cases have raised alarms about violence against trans people in São Luís. Movement leaders in Maranhão say they are closely monitoring the incidents and will demand immediate action from authorities.
> 
> "We, as an organized society, are very concerned. We have already contacted the State Council and the State Forum, where we will urgently request a meeting with the Secretary of Public Security, because we need to find partners to combat this violence against our community..." said Raíssa Mendonça, human rights defender, psychologist, and trans activist.
> 
> A survey by the National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals shows that crimes against trans people in Brazil are committed using a variety of methods, many of them lethal and motivated by hate.
> 
> While the Civil Police continue investigating the deaths of Amanda Falcão and Grampola, social movements have reinforced that the fight goes beyond individual justice. It's about defending the right to life and dignity.
> 
> "We need to do this because as long as we remain silent, the entire system will think everything is fine. But today it was Grampola, yesterday it was Amanda, tomorrow it could be any of us, any of us who are vulnerable. We don't know where this hatred comes from, but we will not be silent, we will not be silenced, nor will we give up fighting for justice for all of us," declared Raíssa.


> " Funny " and " irreverent " - this is how the trans digital influencer ' Grampola ', 41 years old, known in Maranhão as the ' Queen of Audios ', was considered by her followers on social media. She was murdered on her birthday , in the early hours of this Friday (12), in Residencial Ribeira, in São Luís . The Civil Police are investigating the case.
> 
> Grampola was a trans woman and became known throughout Maranhão for the humorous audios she shared on messaging apps and social media . Because of this, her followers nicknamed her “ Queen of Audios ” ( see some of the audios below ).
> 
> On social media, Grampola posted comedy videos , relived old audios that went viral on messaging apps , and promoted products . With her irreverence and charisma, she gained a legion of followers.
> 
> The influencer began her career in the Liberdade neighborhood, but a few years ago, she lived in Residencial Ribeira, in São Luís. After news of her death broke, fans and admirers left countless messages of condolence on her profile.
