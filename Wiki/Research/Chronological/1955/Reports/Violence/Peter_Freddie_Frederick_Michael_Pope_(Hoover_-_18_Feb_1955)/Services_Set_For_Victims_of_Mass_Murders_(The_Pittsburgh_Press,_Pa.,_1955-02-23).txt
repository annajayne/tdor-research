===== Services Set For Victims of Mass Murders =====

==== Sadistic Killer's Funeral Tomorrow ====

UNIONTOWN, Feb. 23 (Special) The first of five victims of a sadistic killer was buried today in the churchyard of his home town.

Funeral services for Steve Frank Demko, 26, were held at 9:30 a. m. today in the Yoney Funeral Home, Masontown, followed by high mass of requiem at St. Marys Church, Leckrone. Burial was in the church cemetery.

Tomorrow, the scene will be duplicated as two more victims of Robert L. Miller, 42, are laid to rest In the same cemetery.

=== Double Services ===

His wife, Helen, 49, and her daughter, Delores, 16, will be buried in adjoining graves after double services at 9 a. m. 

Simultaneously, in Duquesne, requiem high mass will be sung for 17-year-old Peter Frederick Pope.

The Duquesne High School senior is survived by his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Peter Pope, of 33 Earl St., a sister, Jean, 14, and two brothers, Gerard and Barry.

=== Other Rites ===

Friends are being received at the Lemak Funeral Home, 400 W. Grant Ave., Duquesne.

Requiem high mass will be sung at 9 a. m. In St. Joseph's Church, Duquesne. Burial will follow in the church cemetery.

The body of Robert L. Miller, who killed his victims with a hatchet, a hammer and a .25 caliber revolver, will be buried tomorrow afternoon. Services will be at 2 p. m. in the Galley Funeral Home at Dawson, with burial in Flat-wood Cemetery.

The sole survivor of the slayings, Anthony Rivak, 34, who was wounded as he fled from Miller, is reported in good condition at Uniontown Hospital.
