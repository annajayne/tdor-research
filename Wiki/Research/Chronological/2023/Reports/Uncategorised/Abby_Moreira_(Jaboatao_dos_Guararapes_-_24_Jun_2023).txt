> Abby Moreira, first trans woman to become a municipal guard, dies at age 46 in Jaboatão dos Guararapes
> According to the city hall, the employee, who was assigned to the Human Rights Secretariat, had a sudden illness while sleeping. In 2022, she reported that she suffered transphobia in the corporation.
> By g1 PE
> 
> 06/24/2023 6:12 pm Updated3 months ago
> 
> The first trans woman to take on the position of municipal guard in the country, in 2017, Abby Silva Moreira died this Saturday (24), aged 46, in Jaboatão dos Guararapes , in Greater Recife . According to the city hall, she had a sudden illness while sleeping at her home, in the Candeias neighborhood. The cause of death will be investigated by the Death Verification Service (SVO).
> 
> Abby was a member of the Municipal Guard and was assigned to the municipality's Human Rights Department. According to the city hall, the family reported that she went up at night to drink water, went back to sleep and never woke up.
> 
> The municipal management said it was informed by relatives at around 8:30 am. Abby is survived by her wife of 28 years and one son.
> 
> The first and only trans woman to take on the position of municipal guard in Jaboatão dos Guararapes, Abby was removed from street activities in February 2022 and reported that she had been suffering from transphobia. At the time, she told g1 that her salary bonuses were cut, even though she was a public servant.
> 
> Abby also reported that she was undergoing treatment for depression and the condition has worsened since 2021, due to the transphobia of which she was a victim and which resulted in two psychiatric hospitalizations. Following the complaint, the then commander of the Jaboatão Municipal Guard, Admilson Silva de Freitas, was dismissed .
> 
> Abby Moreira was a campaigner for the rights of the LGBTQIA+ population and on her path to living as a trans woman she faced several prejudices and many challenges.
> 
> In 2017, he participated in a campaign by the NGO Gestos - Seropositividade, Comunicação e Gênero for the rights of the LGBTQIA+ population. In a podcast recorded for the "Gestos pela Igualdade" campaign, Abby talked about the journey until she was able to live her gender identity.
> 
> Born in Rio de Janeiro, from a religious family, Abby said that since she was 4 years old she knew that her body did not reflect her identity, but it was only in 2014 that she was able to begin the gender transition process. "I never saw myself as a man, I always saw myself as a woman," she said.
> 
> Abby also said that it took her a while to accept herself. She married at age 18, still living as a male-in a relationship that continued throughout her life. With a degree in business administration, Abby said that she was a businesswoman (owner of a printing press), wrote poetry and worked as a municipal guard in Jaboatão dos Guararapes, a position she took on after her gender transition.
