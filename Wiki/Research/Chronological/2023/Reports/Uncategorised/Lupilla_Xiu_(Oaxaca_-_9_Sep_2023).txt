> Guadalupe Xiu, better known as Lupilla, died, she was an indigenous Oaxacan woman, trans woman, displaced, victim of torture, kidnapping and uprisings by the government of Oaxaca and Mexico City. Farewell September 13 from 9:00 to 11:00 am



> This morning, members of the LGBTIQ+ community held a body mass outside the Ministry of the Interior , to pay tribute to the young trans activist Guadalupe Xiu , 36, who died on Saturday, September 9 due to possible medical complications during a protest. in the vicinity of the same federal building.
> 
> On this topic, human rights defender Kenya Cuevas explained that there was a delay in the hospital medical care of Guadalupe Xiu , because the ambulance arrived at the site almost two hours after the emergency was reported. Cuevas added that once admitted to the Gregorio Salas Hospital, death occurred.
> 
> The founder of the Casa de las Muñecas Tiresias shelter, also recalled that Guadalupe Xiu denounced, at different times, harassment and persecution allegedly carried out by authorities of the state of Oaxaca.
> 
> The young trans activist, Guadalupe Xiu, died from medical complications during a protest she was carrying out outside the Ministry of the Interior.
> PHOTO: ONCE NEWS
> Kenya Cuevas added that as the days passed, Guadalupe remained homeless and faced the challenges that this entails alone.
> 
> “She never had a forceful, clear response from the authorities and this led her to be in this situation. (…) The demands for immediate health services is always an issue, not just now, I think it is an issue that all Mexicans have complained about, that ambulances do not arrive, that when you request them, if the person It is not fatally serious, so they do not take it,” denounced Kenya Cuevas, Founder of Casa de las Muñecas Tiresias .
> 
> In April 2022, supporters of Guadalupe Xiu denounced that the undersecretary of human rights, of the Ministry of the Interior, Alejandro Encinas, had participated in the forced disappearance of the young woman.
> 
> However, the official immediately emphasized that this version was false.
> 
> Through a statement released on that date, the Ministry of the Interior detailed that Guadalupe was in Oaxaca, where at her request, the federal agency coordinated actions to request hearings with the state government and to request identity recognition procedures. gender to exercise their rights as a trans woman.




> They hold a demonstration in front of Segob with the body of Guadalupe Xiu, trans activist
> The transgender activist, Guadalupe Xiu, died on Sunday and his family decided to hold a last demonstration with his body present in front of Segob.
> 
> Published: 09/13/2023 | ?? 10:23| Updated ?? 11:13
> 
> Around 9:00 in the morning this Wednesday , Guadalupe Xiu 's family went to the vicinity of the Ministry of the Interior ( Segob ) to hold a last demonstration with the body of the transgender activist , who died last Sunday after of a long fight for their rights and those of their community.
> 
> The peaceful protest took place outside the building located on Abraham González Street No. 48, in the Juárez neighborhood of the Cuauhtémoc mayor's office . In that same place, Guadalupe appeared on multiple occasions to request security and support, after reporting having suffered torture and kidnapping in Oaxaca , in addition to a series of violations of her human rights.
> 
> This September 13 , the activist's body was present inside her coffin, covered and accompanied by her family, who filled the place with flowers and prayers while a mass was held for her rest.
> 
> Guadalupe Xiu , who was known as “Lupilla,” held multiple protests in front of the Ministry of the Interior and reported having been displaced from her community of origin in Oaxaca . In recent years, she held various sit-ins outside the building and demanded support from the authorities to receive medical attention and shelter.
