> Who was Ruddys Martínez and what did “La Pantoja de Puerto Rico” die of?
> Learn more about the life and career of the unforgettable “Mami Ruddys”, whose death has shocked the world of entertainment.
> 11/28/2023 04:34 a.m. m.
> Unfortunately, we must report that another star has gone out. Last Sunday, November 26, Ruddys Martínez , affectionately known in Spain as “La Pantoja de Puerto Rico”, died at the age of 62. Here we offer you a look at the life of this renowned trans artist and details about the circumstances of her death.
> 
> Ruddy herself shared the news of her upcoming departure with her followers through social media, leaving a moving message hours before her death.
> 
> “ I am writing to you from my transition, my heart could not stand so much love and from where I am I will try to open new doors that I do not know. Those who love me will remember me and have me in their hearts... as I always told them: 'You have to take care of yourself! They will put my body so they can say goodbye to me, coconuts...' ”, he expressed on Facebook.
> 
> 
> Ruddys Martínez Díaz was a prominent queer icon from Puerto Rico, recognized for his innovative work in drag and his charisma on stage, accumulating more than 40 years of artistic career.
> 
> Her fame transcended borders when she captivated the Spanish public with her imitations in Telecinco's “Crónicas Marcianas” in the early 2000s, with Mami Ruddys being one of her most emblematic characters.
> 
> Her legacy is not just limited to entertainment, as Ruddys was also a respected activist in the trans community. Her humanitarian work was notable, providing shelter to more than 30 people rejected by their families due to her sexual orientation.
> 
> Ruddys Martínez died due to complications derived from his diabetes, after spending several days admitted to the Doctor Center hospital in Bayamón, Puerto Rico.
> 
> The artist faced various health problems during her hospitalization on November 21, and the official cause of her death was a serious pulmonary complication.
> 
> WHAT IS THE DIABETES?
> According to the specialized media Mayo Clinic , diabetes mellitus is a group of diseases that affect the way the body uses blood sugar, called glucose.
> 
> Glucose is essential for providing energy to cells, including muscle and brain cells. Diabetes can occur when there is too much sugar in the blood, which can cause serious health problems.
> 
> Yes, in severe and uncontrolled cases, diabetes can have life-threatening consequences. Long-term complications of diabetes can affect various organs and systems of the body, including the heart, kidneys, nerves, blood vessels or – in the case of Ruddys Martínez – the lungs.
> 
> Additionally, extreme fluctuations in blood sugar levels can lead to medical emergencies such as diabetic ketoacidosis (in people with type 1 diabetes) or hyperosmolar hyperglycemic syndrome (in people with type 2 diabetes), both serious conditions that can endanger life.
