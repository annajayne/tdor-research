> A young trans woman was shot dead at the door of a friend's house in the Vergel do Lago neighborhood, this Monday (11), in Maceió. The 18-year-old victim, identified only as Louise, had just left school 
> 
> According to information from the Military Police, the crime was committed by individuals who arrived at the scene in a Toyota Hilux vehicle.
> 
> One of his men allegedly got out of the car, with a balaclava covering his face, and shot the young woman four times.
> 
> According to information gathered by Ronda Antena, Louise was a drug user, but was not involved in crime. The police are investigating whether the crime was motivated by passion.
> 
> Teams from the Homicide and Personal Protection Police Station (DHPP) were at the scene.

> An 18-year-old trans woman was brutally murdered with four shots to the head on Monday night (11), in the Vergel do Lago neighborhood, in Maceió. The case is investigated by the Civil Police.
> 
> According to information from the Military Police, the victim, Louise Melo, was with another person going home, when they were surprised by a man wearing a balaclava (a hat that covers the head). 
> 
> After shooting Louise four times in the head, the man fled. The victim succumbed to his injuries and died. To date, the author has not been located.
> 
> Anyone with information about his whereabouts can contact the Civil Police Hotline 181 . The call is free and anonymity is guaranteed to the complainant.
> 
> Forensics attended the scene, and the body was sent to the IML. The Homicide and Personal Protection Police Department (DHPP) was also at the scene and is expected to investigate the case.



> A trans woman identified as Louise Melo was murdered with shots to the head on her way home in the Vergel do Lago neighborhood, in Maceió, on Monday night (11).
> 
> The 18-year-old was approached by a man wearing a balaclava, who shot her four times and fled. A person who was with the victim witnessed the crime.
> 
> Louise died at the scene and the body was collected by a team from the Legal Medical Institute (IML) of Maceió.
> 
> The Homicide and Personal Protection Department (DHPP) investigates the authorship and motivation of the crime.
