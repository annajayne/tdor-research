> Who was the trans woman murdered and tied to a tree in Bogotá? This is what the authorities say
> The woman, originally from Barrancabermeja, Santander, was recognized for defending human rights in the region, particularly those of trans women.
> 
> December 15, 2023 By: El País Editorial Team
> This Thursday, December 14, a trans woman was murdered, hung and tied by her hands and feet to a tree in the Eastern Hills of Bogotá. This fact has generated shock and rejection from the Lgbtq + community, since the victim was a social leader in Santander, where she defended the humans of the region.
> 
> This is Roxana Delgado, 32 years old, from the municipality of Barrancabermeja, Santander, where she left, apparently, due to threats and intimidation; She arrived in the Colombian capital a few years ago, looking for new opportunities.
>

> Iván Prada Serrano from the Lgbtiq collective, spoke about the murdered woman: “She was well known in the region, she was an activist for the community, especially for trans women, so that their rights were recognized.”
> 
> Roxana Delgado was a native of Barrancabermeja, Santander, where she fled due to alleged threats.
> Roxana Delgada, was a social leader recognized in Santander for defending human rights in the region. | Photo: Taken from social networks
> “The living conditions of these women have a stigma and they left the region precisely because they suffered from violence, and apparently because of threats, and that is why they moved to the capital of the country looking for a better quality of care. life”, added the spokesperson.
> 
> As indicated by the authorities to the Lgbtiq community, this murder could have occurred due to their gender identity and violence due to prejudice. A fact that those close to Roxana hope will see justice done.
> 
> It is important to mention that with this crime there are 24 trans women murdered in Colombia during 2023.
> 
> Roxana's crime
> The 32-year-old trans woman was found lifeless and tied by her hands and feet to a tree in the Cerros Orientales, in the town of Santa Fe, on a road that leads to Cerro de Guadalupe.
> 
> According to the authorities, Roxana Delgado died from mechanical asphyxiation, a crime that is being investigated and the Forensic Medicine report is pending.
> 
> Roxana Delgado was a native of Barrancabermeja, Santander, where she fled due to alleged threats.

> “The quadrant reports that a person has been murdered, there is mechanical asphyxiation, the body is in a wooded area, tied to a tree, mobile phones are under investigation, inspection is being carried out, Síjin Laboratory,” specifies the first report from the authorities.



> During the morning of this Thursday, December 14, it was learned about the murder of a trans woman who, apparently, was asphyxiated and hanged of a tree in the eastern hills of Bogotá. 
> 
> According to the authorities' preliminary report, the events occurred around 8:45 in the morning in the town of Santa Fe, on the road that leads to Guadalupe Hill.
> 
> The woman, who was identified as 32-year-old Roxana Delgado, had signs of asphyxiation and a rope was tied to her neck. At the time of discovery, the woman's body was hanging from the trunk of a tree.
> 
> "The quadrant informs that the homicide of a person occurs, there is mechanical asphyxiation, the body is found in a wooded area, tied to a tree, mobile phones are under investigation, Síjin Laboratory is inspecting," reads the first report from the authorities.
> 
> In the images of the events it is also possible to show that the woman had been tied by her hands. For now, investigators are working to establish the motives for the events and to reconstruct the crime scene in order to find those responsible for the macabre murder.
