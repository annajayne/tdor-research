> PESHAWAR: Unknown attackers shot dead a transgender person near the Government College Chowk in Hashtnagri locality of the provincial capital on Tuesday afternoon, the police said.
> 
> An official of Gulfat Hussain Shaheed police station identified the slain as Noor Mohammad alias Ubaiday, an Afghan national, who was currently living in the Patang Chowk area of the provincial capital.
> 
> The official said it was not immediately clear as to who murdered the transgender, and they had yet to lodge an FIR.
> 
> On the other hand, a police statement said unknown motorcyclists opened fire on the transgender person near the Government College Chowk. It said the police personnel shifted the body to hospital for autopsy.
> 
> The statement said no identity documents were found on the body. Besides, it said police had collected empties of bullets used in the attack and were also collecting the CCTV footages from the area.
> 
> However, a police source told Dawn that an accused identified as Babar Ali opened fire on the transgender person. He said the accused was a partner of the slain and police investigators had also found his pictures in Ubaidayâ€™s room.
> 
> This is the second murder of a transgender person in the provincial capital in over a month. Earlier on Nov 10, a transgender individual identified as Ghazal was stabbed to death in the limits of Faqeerabad police station.

> On the evening of December 26, 2023, the streets of Peshawar, specifically near Government College Chowk in Faqirabad, were the backdrop to a chilling crime. A transgender individual fell victim to an act of violence, brutally shot and killed by unidentified motorcyclists. The incident has shocked the community and prompted calls for urgent action and reform.
> 
> The Superintendent of Police (SP) for Faqirabad Division, Osama Amin Cheema, immediately arrived at the scene, where he coordinated the collection of critical evidence, including empty shells. The police have launched an investigation, exploring all possible leads. The body of the deceased was transferred to the hospital for a thorough autopsy, a grim but necessary step in the quest for justice.
> 
> Following the incident, a city-wide manhunt was instigated. The police, armed with CCTV footage from the surrounding area, are tirelessly working to identify and apprehend the suspects. The footage, while grainy, provides a tangible link to the assailants and an invaluable tool in their pursuit.
> 
> The deceased, whose identity remains anonymous for their safety, was residing in a plaza near a private university. The incident raises grave concerns about the safety and rights of the transgender community in the region. It amplifies the need for stronger protection measures, societal acceptance, and equal rights for this vulnerable segment of society.
