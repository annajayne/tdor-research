> A tragic event has shaken the community of El Guamo and the LGTBI community in general, upon hearing the news of the death of María Paula Rodríguez, affectionately known as "Cristal". María Paula, an active member of the LGTBI community and originally from the Chontaduro village in El Guamo, was found dead in the city of Bogotá, in circumstances that are being investigated by the authorities.
> 
> The news of his loss has plunged his native community into a deep state of sadness and dismay. Messages of condolence and support have been sent to her grieving mother, who is facing a time of indescribable pain.
> 
> María Paula Rodríguez was a young woman who left a deep mark on the lives of her loved ones and on the LGTBI community. Her tragic departure has left a void impossible to fill and has generated a wave of grief among her friends, family and colleagues.
> 
> While authorities work tirelessly to shed light on the circumstances surrounding her death, the LGTBI community and those who knew María Paula remember her with affection and gratitude for her contribution to the fight for equality and the rights of the community.
> 
> The LGTBI community hopes that justice will be done in the case of María Paula Rodríguez and reiterates its commitment to the fight against discrimination and violence that affects this community.
> 
> In this moment of pain, from Ecos del Combeima we send wishes of peace and comfort to the loved ones of María Paula Rodríguez, 'Cristal'.



> Violence against LGTBI population: body of transgender woman found in Chapinero hotel
> The police found the body in a hotel room, in the Pardo Rubio neighborhood. The body had several stab wounds and was wrapped in sheets.
> November 8, 2023 - 03:48 pm
> The authorities arrived at the sector guided by the murderer himself.
> 
> On the morning of Sunday, November 5, authorities from the town of Chapinero arrived at the crime scene, regarding a call received by line 123. When they entered the hotel, located on 52nd Street and 3rd Street. The workers there They were told that in one of the rooms on the second floor a woman was lying who, apparently, was lifeless. The crime would have occurred in the early morning hours.
> 
> The police found the victim in room 206. The body had several wounds caused by sharp objects and was wrapped in a sheet. Once they confirmed that she was dead, they notified the CTI of the Prosecutor's Office to begin the work of removing the body.
> 
> “What is known so far is that it is a transgender woman, approximately 50 years old, who entered the hotel hours before the events occurred. The motives for the crime have not been established,” a local police officer, who was familiar with the case, told El Espectador.
> 
> There are no clues about the possible murderer, who is only known to be a younger man than the victim. Detectives are analyzing security camera videos to try to determine the identity of the person who accompanied the victim and the exact time he left the hotel. For now, the identity of the victim has not been revealed by authorities.
