> Rotam finds young people digging graves and locates the body of a transvestite in Sarandi
> Theddy Gonçalves November 24, 2023
> 
> A Rotam team from Sarandi caught a group of young people digging a grave in the middle of a coffee plantation in the Jardim Ana Eliza neighborhood, in Sarandi, on Thursday night, November 23rd.  
> 
> The police officers were carrying out routine patrols on Rua Barão de Mauá, when they saw individuals leaving the coffee plantation and jumping over the wall of a residence. The group was approached and upon realizing that the individuals were covered in mud, the police went to check the site and located a dead person wrapped in a tarpaulin. 
> 
> Next to the body, a shallow grave, a shovel and a bag of lime were found. Occurrence in Progress.






> The family buried the body of Carlos Raphael Tavares Camargo, aged 22, in the Marialva Municipal Cemetery. The victim was found dead on the night of Thursday, 23rd, in the middle of a plantation in Jardim Ana Eliza, in Sarandi.
> 
> The Military Police only found the body because they saw suspects covered in mud leaving a field. The men had just dug a grave, possibly to bury the victim's body.
> 
> The transvestite's body was already wrapped in a black tarpaulin. The police also seized lime that would be used to speed up the decomposition of the corpse. The suspects were arrested and taken to the Sarandi police station.
> 
> An autopsy examination carried out at the IML identified gunshot wounds in the chest and abdomen. Carlos liked people to call him 'Alejandra'. In January of this year, Alejandra had 40% of her body burned after a fight in Praça dos Expedicionarios.
> 
> A 24-year-old transvestite set Alejandra's body on fire because of jealousy. The victim asked for help at a medical clinic on Avenida Rio Branco. The perpetrator of the crime was arrested minutes later by a PM team.
