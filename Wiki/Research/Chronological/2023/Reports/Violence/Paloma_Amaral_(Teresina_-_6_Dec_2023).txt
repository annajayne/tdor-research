> 11/12/23, 16:21
> Transvestite who was tortured and placed in a trunk is murdered with blows to the head
> 
> Paloma Amaral, 34 years old, was beaten to death with sticks to the head in the center of Teresina. The body was found by members of the public and taken to the Teresina Emergency Hospital (HUT). Two years ago, Paloma was the target of other crimes, when she was tortured, had her feet tied and placed in the trunk of a car. The crime gained repercussion for its cruelty.
> 
> According to Cidadeverde.com , Paloma had been at Casa do Caminho, a shelter that welcomes homeless people, in the center of Teresina for three months, when she left to go to Caps (Psychosocial Care Center) and did not return.
> 
> The aunt reported that Paloma was found with several blows to the head. She was admitted to the HUT last Wednesday, but it was only this Sunday (10) that the family recognized her body, four days after the crime.
> 
> Marinalva Santana, from the Matizes group, said that Paloma always came to her asking for help and assuring her that she had stopped using drugs, after the death of her mother, who passed away around two months ago.   
> 
> “She said she had promised on her mother's coffin that she wouldn't use drugs anymore and she was clean the last time I saw her, which was the 30th. She had asked me for help. I booked a dentist as she needed dental work and had gotten some clothes. But I made an appointment with her and she didn't show up. I thought it was strange and called some people and found out that she was murdered”, said Marinalva.
> 
> According to Marinalva Santana, it is yet another crime of transphobia , the third since last month.
> 
> “It’s another hate crime against LGBTQIA+. They are not content with shooting, stabbing, they have to kill with cruelty. Her sister reported that Paloma’s head was destroyed due to the cruelty of the violence.”
> 
> The family filed a police report. The body remains at the IML and will be buried tomorrow at 10:30 am at the Santa Cruz cemetery, in the Promorar neighborhood, in the south of Teresina. 
> 
> André Santos, from the Municipal Human Rights Council, reported that Paloma was being monitored by psychologists and had signed up to change her social name. 
> 
> “She had already been monitored by the coordinator for a long time, as she had experienced a violation of her rights. We asked the authorities for the crime to be investigated, we contacted the family and made ourselves available for anything they needed, as they were sheltered in a shelter in the municipality.
