> Death of trans woman is investigated in Jaboatão; body was on the banks of a stream
> According to police, case registered in Engenho Velho is treated as a death to be clarified
> By: Diario de Pernambuco
> Published on: 11/16/2023 10:32 | Updated on: 11/16/2023 11:06
> 
> The Department of Homicide and Personal Protection (DHPP) is investigating yet another death of a trans woman in Greater Recife. 
> 
> The victim's body was found on the banks of a stream, in the neighborhood of Engenho Velho, in Jaboatão dos Guararapes, in the Metropolitan Region of Recife.
> 
> The case happened on Wednesday (15), but was confirmed by the Civil Police of Pernambuco (PCPE) this Thursday (16). 
> 
> The victim, preliminarily identified only as Hayalla, aged 25, was found dead by residents of the area, who called the police. 
> 
> The case was registered at the Prazeres Police Station. According to the police press office, the case is treated as “a death to be clarified”. 
> 
> The corporation did not provide further details about the case, but stated that an inquiry was opened to investigate the case. 
> 
> After examination carried out by experts from the Institute of Criminalistics (IC), the victim's body was sent to the Institute of Legal Medicine (IML), in the Santo Amaro neighborhood, in the Central area of Recife. 
> 
> On October 18, the police began investigating the death of Evelin Sofia da Silva, 24 years old.
> 
> The trans woman was shot dead inside her residence, in the Afogados neighborhood, in the North Zone of Recife. 
> 
> According to PCPE, she was found by military police officers lifeless and with several gunshot wounds.
> 
> The crime took place in the community of Caranguejo Tabaiares, on the border between Ilha do Retiro and Afogados. 
> 
> The victim worked as a trader and produced hot lunches to sell in the locality.
> 
> The Capital Homicide Task Force and the Criminalistics Institute (IC) were at the scene to carry out the first investigations into the crime and the examination of the victim's body. 
> 
> Pernambuco led, in 2022, the ranking of murders of trans people across the country.
> 
> The data is from the National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals (Antra).
> 
> The dossier showed that the state recorded 13 cases of Intentional Lethal Violent Crimes (CVLIs) against trans people. 
> 
> According to the entity, at the time, Pernambuco went from 5th in the ranking to take first place.
> 
> The state was followed by São Paulo, which fell from 1st to 2nd position, and Ceará, which went from 4th to 3rd, in 2022, with 11 cases each.
> 
> Minas Gerais maintained the nine cases from the previous year and Rio de Janeiro, which had 12 murders in 2021, closed 2022 with eight. 
> 
> Amazonas had eight cases and Bahia 7. Paraná, Pará and Espírito Santo had six murders each; Goiás, Mato Grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul and Sergipe with five cases each.
> 
> These states were followed by Alagoas, Paraíba and Maranhão with four murders in each state and Rio Grande do Norte with 3.
> 
> There were also two cases in Rio Grande do Sul, Santa Catarina and the Federal District. A murder in Rondônia, Piauí and Roraima.
