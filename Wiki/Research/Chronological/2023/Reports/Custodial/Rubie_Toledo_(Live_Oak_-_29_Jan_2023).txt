> Live Oak, FL - Rubie Toledo, a transgender woman, died of a fentanyl overdose while incarcerated in a men’s facility at Suwannee Correctional Institution. I’ve tried to find a photo of her that isn’t a mugshot and honors her gender but so far this is the best photo I could find, assuming this is her. Her family is suing.
> Rubie Toledo “was a transgender woman incarcerated at Suwannee C.I., a men's prison, prior to her death. She had a history of asthma, hearing impairment, adverse drug reaction, post-traumatic stress disorder, seizure disorders, and other medical and mental health issues. FDC was on notice of these issues prior to
> Mx. Toledo's death.
> 
> At a previous institution Mx. Toledo was held in protective management, a classification status reserved for high-risk prisoners. At Suwannee C.I.-where Mx.
> 
> Toledo was housed from November 30, 2022 until her death on January 29, 2023—Mx. Toledo was placed in Close Management 1 and 2 (CM-1 and -2) status for possession of drugs at a prior institution.
> 
13. > At Suwannee C.I., Mx. Toledo was assigned various cellmates (though she never shared a cell within anyone else at Suwannee C.I. because the male prisoners refused to be celled with Mx. Toledo). In fact, Mx. Toledo was a target in men's prisons because of her transgender identity, and she was also a target of gang
> threats and violence, which FDC knew prior to her arrival at Suwannee C.I.
> 
> Mx. Toledo also grieved her conditions at Suwannee C.I., including lack of access to her hormone therapy and shaving materials, which she expressed were causing her depression. She also claimed that staff at Suwannee C.I. were throwing away her grievances and asked Plaintiff Marlene Toledo and others to reach out to the
> warden and Secretary about these issues. Before they could, Mx. Toledo died.”



> TALLAHASSEE, Fla. (Legal Newsline)  - Staff at a Florida men's jail failed to adequately monitor a transgender woman inmate there, says a lawsuit over her fentanyl-related death.
> 
> Marlene Toledo, as the personal representative of the estate of Ruben Zachary Toledo, on July 10 in Leon County Circuit Court brought a wrongful death claim against the Florida Department of Corrections (FDC). 
> 
> The complaint alleges that FDC's negligence led to the death of Ruben (Rubie) Toledo, a transgender woman who was incarcerated at Suwannee Correctional Institution. 
> 
> The suit claims that FDC failed to adequately monitor and ensure the safety of Mx. Toledo despite being aware of her medical and mental health issues and previous threats due to her transgender identity. The plaintiff seeks damages exceeding $50,000 for wrongful death under Florida law.
